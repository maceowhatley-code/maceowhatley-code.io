The Long Vision

Journalism is not broken because people stopped caring.
It is broken because journalists were never in charge.

Newsrooms are closing. Journalists are underpaid, laid off, or pressured into chasing clicks to survive. The stories that matter most — the investigations, the accountability reporting, the inconvenient truths — are the ones least likely to get funded. Not because audiences don't want them, but because the people paying for media have always been advertisers, not readers.

IYK.YK is a new model. A community-governed institution where journalists get paid directly for the work the public actually needs. Where reporters and contributors have a real stake in the platform they're building. Where the archive of what gets published belongs to no single owner and can never be taken down.
"What if the journalists owned the institution — and the community funded it directly?"
Why 2026
The infrastructure finally exists.
The old system is finally failing.
This could not have been built in 2021. The tools were speculative. The networks were thin. The legal structures for community-governed organizations did not exist in any workable form.

Three things converged in 2025 that make 2026 the right moment. First, the collapse of legacy media is no longer gradual — it is structural. BuzzFeed News is gone. Vice is bankrupt. Local newsrooms are closing at a rate of two per week across the United States. The people who believed the old model would stabilize have run out of time to wait.

Second, Farcaster has reached a critical mass of journalists, builders, and media professionals who are not crypto-native speculators — they are people who left broken institutions and are looking for something worth building. The audience for what IYK.YK is offering exists, and it is reachable.

Third, the infrastructure stack — blank.space for governance, Octant for yield-based funding, on-chain reputation via soulbound tokens — is now deployable without custom engineering. The barrier is no longer technical. It is organizational. That is a gap people can fill.
What Has Been Tried
Others have tried. Their failures
are the blueprint for this design.
Proof it can work
Defector Media (2020) — Eighteen journalists left a dying publication, launched a worker-owned company, and built a sustainable audience of 35,000+ paying subscribers. It proved that journalist ownership is viable and that audiences will follow reporters they trust. IYK.YK takes that model further: the community owns it, not just the journalists.
Proved community treasury can fund media
Bankless DAO (2021) — Demonstrated that a community treasury could fund ongoing media production at scale. Its limitation: no editorial standards, no quality filter, and governance capture by insiders over time. IYK.YK uses competitions and soulbound reputation to solve exactly that problem.
Why the last attempt failed
Civil (2017–2020) — Raised $5 million to build a blockchain-based journalism platform. Shut down operations by 2020. The failure was not the vision — it was the model. Token economics were speculative. There was no mechanism for sustainable, yield-based funding. Editorial quality was inconsistent because anyone could publish. IYK.YK is designed around every one of those failure points: endowment yield over token speculation, competition-based quality filtering, and a permanent archive that is not dependent on platform survival.

How IYK.YK Works
Ownership
Members govern the institution. Those who contribute most earn more say in how funds are directed. Your track record as a contributor — whether you deliver, collaborate, and show up — is recorded permanently and belongs to you. Governance decentralizes by design.
Getting Paid
New contributors earn membership by winning open competitions. Existing members earn rewards for every piece they publish. The institution is funded through community investment — and runs on yield only, so the founding capital is never depleted. The treasury belongs to the community, permanently.
The Archive
Every story, question, and submission is archived permanently — organized, searchable, and owned by no single platform. AI helps synthesize what the community is asking and what the journalism is answering. The record of this institution cannot be deleted, bought, or buried.
Sustainability Model

IYK.YK runs on an endowment model. Auction proceeds and patron stakes flow into an Octant vault. Only yield is deployed — the principal is preserved permanently. External patrons stake directly into the IYK.YK vault, growing the yield base independently of auction cadence. The more compelling the journalism, the more people stake.

How It Sustains Itself
Community investment seeds the fund
Supporters stake, the fund grows
Returns fund journalist competitions
Great journalism attracts more supporters
The institution sustains itself — permanently

Being Related
The unseen foundation of all accomplishment. Must exist before the game begins. The larger this foundation, the larger what can be built. Possibility naturally falls out of it.
Possibility
Not prediction — declaration. Rich enough to draw the present toward it. Alive in listening and speaking. Distinct from a pipe dream, which invents a future people cannot hear.
Opportunity
Translates possibility into something feasible and do-able. Creates the Who, What, Where, When from nothing. Without it, commitment weakens. There are no opportunities in life — they are a conversation.
Action
Requests, promises, declarations — not to-dos. "I request X by Y." "I promise X by Y." There is no try. A decline is also a promise. Conditions of satisfaction and a deadline are required.
Completion
Not merely finished — whole, with integrity, nothing missing. Ask "What is incomplete?" until the answer is nothing. Standing in completion is what lets you see what still needs completing.
Breakdown
A disruption to a commitment — declared, not reacted to. "I declare a breakdown in X." Not a problem. A breakdown is yours to declare and yours to resolve. Bigger commitments produce bigger breakdowns.
Breakdown Protocol
When things do not go as planned, stop immediately. Declare the breakdown — "I declare a breakdown in X." Get into a conversation for possibility and opportunity. Make requests and promises to resolve it. Do not manage around it. Update this document to reflect what happened and what the next steps are.
Multiple Conversations
Any team can be inside multiple conversations simultaneously. Tags shown each week reflect all conversations present — not a single dominant one. Being Related is always in the background. A breakdown can arise inside any other conversation.

Glossary — For Those New to This Territory
DAO
Decentralized Autonomous Organization. A community that governs itself through transparent rules written into code, rather than through a CEO or board. Decisions are made collectively by members, and the rules cannot be changed by any single person.
NFT (Membership Token)
A Non-Fungible Token. In IYK.YK, NFTs are not digital art — they are membership credentials. Winning a competition earns you one. Holding one gives you a vote in how the institution is governed and how its funds are allocated.
ERC-20 (Contributor Token)
A type of digital token earned by contributing — writing, editing, producing. Think of it as the currency of the platform. It is how active contributors get paid for their work and earn a say in operational decisions.
Soulbound Token (Reputation)
A permanent, non-transferable record of your contributions — it cannot be bought, sold, or faked. It answers the question: do you deliver what you promise? Have you collaborated well? Your reputation here belongs to you and travels with you.
Nouns / blank.space
Nouns is a model for community governance where new membership tokens are auctioned continuously, funding a shared treasury. blank.space is a platform built on this model, designed specifically for community-governed organizations. It is where IYK.YK lives.
Octant Vault
A funding mechanism where community investment is pooled and staked to generate yield — similar to how an endowment works. IYK.YK deploys only the returns, never the principal. The founding investment is preserved permanently and governed by the community.
Farcaster
A decentralized social network — like Twitter, but no single company owns it. IYK.YK uses Farcaster as its public-facing channel, where journalists, contributors, and the community discover and engage with the platform.
Knowledge Graph / Bonfires
A structured, interconnected archive of everything the institution produces and asks — stories, questions, sources, and context — organized so that relationships between ideas are visible and searchable. Bonfires is the tool IYK.YK uses to build and maintain this archive onchain.
Bounty / Journalism Competition
A funded prompt — a question or story the community wants answered. Anyone can submit journalism in response. The best submissions win membership and compensation. Bounties are how IYK.YK commissions journalism without a traditional editorial hierarchy deciding what gets covered.
